  3. The Ipod Touch

    1st Generation Ipod Classic by flickr. Image created by Jenne Robinson Faber in 2005.

    In September of 2007 there was a plethora of new iPods and music players coming out but there was one that rose above the rest the iPod touch. The iPod Touch was revolutionary in the world of tech becoming the worlds first true video iPod the world had been waiting for. It gave people the basic feel of a modern day iPhone giving them the browsing power that just a few years before was only accessible from a computer on a mobile device that could fit in their pockets. Unfornuity though on release day it only came with under 32GB of Data. Later that year in October of 2007 apple released an even more vamped out iPod the iPod classic. The Classic and the iPod touch were partners in crime with a lot of the same features that were debuted in the touch reappearing in the classic. However, the classic was pretty much a super iPod touch because on release the Classic had an enormous 160GB storage drive along with a smaller 80GB version that came out in the later months. The Classic also featured more go-faster stripes than the original touch. The first generation also premiered a sleeker and smaller design than the touch, but the classic was almost primarily used as a music listen device and didn’t have many other applications. On the other hand, the iPod touch could be used for many browser uses and was really the corner stone for the future of iPhone and smartphones that we have today.
